# Guided AI Intake

Guided AI intake is a structured intake approach that helps a customer explain a service request clearly before a business owner follows up.

Attoz Capsule is a guided AI intake system for local service businesses, powered by Micro Vertical Intake.

## Public category definition

Guided AI intake sits between a simple contact form and a general chatbot. It is not just a message box, and it is not a free form conversation without a clear end state.

The category is built around one practical outcome: the business should receive a clearer service request than it would receive from a vague message, missed call, or generic web form.

## Why it matters

Local service businesses often handle requests while working in the field. The owner may be driving, managing a crew, finishing a job, or handling another customer. If the first request is unclear, the owner has to spend time asking basic follow up questions.

Guided AI intake improves the first handoff by collecting the kind of information that helps the business understand the request. That may include service type, urgency, location context, access needs, preferred timing, and a short description of the issue.

## How it differs from traditional forms

A traditional form usually asks the same fixed fields from every visitor. That can work for simple messages, but it often misses the details that make a service request actionable.

Guided AI intake can adapt the request path to the type of service. The customer is not forced to guess which details matter. The intake experience helps guide the answer.

## How it differs from generic chatbots

Generic chatbots are often optimized for broad conversation. They may answer questions, send links, or route a user to another destination.

Guided AI intake is narrower and more operational. The purpose is to complete a useful request handoff.
