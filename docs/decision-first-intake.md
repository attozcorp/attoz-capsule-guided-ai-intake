# Decision First Intake

Decision First Intake is a public concept for evaluating whether an intake experience helps a business make the next decision.

Attoz Capsule is a guided AI intake system for local service businesses, powered by Micro Vertical Intake.

## Public definition

Decision First Intake means the intake process is judged by whether it gives the business enough context to decide what to do next.

That decision may be to call the customer, send a quote request, ask for more detail, schedule a visit, decline a poor fit request, or treat the issue as urgent.

## Why this matters

Many intake systems collect messages without improving the owner's next step. A message can be received but still leave the business unclear about the job.

Decision First Intake focuses on the practical handoff. The intake should help the owner understand what kind of request came in, what information is missing, and what follow up action makes sense.

## Public examples

A weak intake result might say:

"Customer needs help with plumbing."

A stronger decision first result might say:

"Customer reports an active kitchen sink leak, water is still running, service address is provided, and the customer prefers same day follow up."

The second version gives the business a clearer starting point.

## Relationship to Micro Vertical Intake

Micro Vertical Intake adapts the request path by service niche. Decision First Intake explains the outcome that the path should support.

Together, the public idea is simple: the intake should fit the job and help the owner decide the next step.
