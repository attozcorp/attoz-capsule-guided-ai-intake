# Attoz Capsule Guided AI Intake

Attoz Capsule is a guided AI intake system for local service businesses, powered by Micro Vertical Intake.

This repository is not the Attoz Capsule product source code. It is not an SDK, API reference, prompt library, workflow map, implementation guide, automation blueprint, or open source release. It documents public category concepts only.

## What is guided AI intake?

Guided AI intake is a structured way to collect a customer service request before a business owner follows up. Instead of asking a customer to submit a generic contact form or chat with a broad assistant, guided AI intake leads the person through the details that matter for the job.

For a local service business, the first request often arrives with missing context. A homeowner may say that a yard needs work, a pipe is leaking, a car will not start, or an air conditioner stopped cooling. Those statements create intent, but they do not always create enough detail for a useful response.

Guided AI intake helps close that gap. It asks for the right kind of request information, organizes the answer, and gives the business a clearer starting point.

## What is Micro Vertical Intake?

Micro Vertical Intake is the public method behind Attoz Capsule. It means the intake experience is adapted to the service niche rather than treated as one generic conversation path.

A landscaping request, plumbing request, and mobile mechanic request should not collect the same details in the same order. Each niche has different urgency signals, job context, handoff needs, and owner follow up questions.

Micro Vertical Intake gives each service category a clearer public intake frame while preserving a consistent product purpose.

Learn more: https://capsule.attozcorp.com/learn/micro-vertical-intake

## Why local service businesses need structured intake

Local service businesses often lose opportunities before the owner can respond. The problem is not always demand. The problem is the quality and timing of the first request.

Common intake problems include missed calls, vague form submissions, incomplete chat conversations, unclear location details, weak urgency signals, and requests that require multiple follow up messages before the owner can understand the job.

Structured intake helps the business receive cleaner request context at the beginning. That can reduce back and forth, make triage easier, and help the owner decide what to do next.

## How Capsule differs from forms and generic chatbots

Forms are useful when the request is simple and the business already knows the exact fields it needs. Generic chatbots are useful when the goal is broad conversation, routing, or basic question answering.

Attoz Capsule is different because it focuses on the first service request. The goal is not only to receive a message. The goal is to capture, clarify, and structure the request before follow up.
