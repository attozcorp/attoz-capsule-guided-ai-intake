# Plumbing Intake Example

This is a public, illustrative example of how guided AI intake can clarify a plumbing request. It does not use real customer data.

Attoz Capsule is a guided AI intake system for local service businesses, powered by Micro Vertical Intake.

## Example request

A customer says there is water under the kitchen sink. That statement matters, but the owner still needs to know whether the situation is active, contained, urgent, or related to a specific fixture.

## Useful intake details

A guided intake path may ask for:

1. Fixture or area affected.
2. Whether water is actively leaking.
3. Whether the customer can shut off water.
4. Severity and urgency.
5. Service address.
6. Access notes.
7. Preferred callback window.
8. Contact details.

## Example owner handoff

"Customer reports an active leak under the kitchen sink. Water is still dripping, but the customer has placed a bucket under the area. Service address is provided. Customer prefers a same day callback."

## Why this proves niche difference

A plumbing request often needs urgency, fixture, water status, and access details. That is different from a landscaping request, where property condition and optional photos may matter more.
